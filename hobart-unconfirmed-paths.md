## Path 2c — Hobart → WashU McKelvey 3:3 (if confirmed: ~$302K, 6 yrs)

### Headline (research as of April 2026)

- **Degree outcome:** BA from Hobart + **BS + MS** in engineering from WashU McKelvey. Six total years.
- **Admission:** Minimum 3.25 GPA (overall and math/science). Per WashU's FAQ, all applicants meeting the GPA, prereq, and institutional-endorsement criteria are admitted (~90% admit rate). Effectively automatic absent a felony or academic integrity violation.
- **Cost:** Automatic **Graduate Affiliation Scholarship** = flat **50% tuition discount** for all 3 years at WashU. At 2026–27 rates ($69,190/yr graduate tuition), that's **~$34,500/yr post-discount × 3 years ≈ $103,500** at WashU. Combined with 3 years at Hobart ($41K/yr scholarship, ~$82K tuition / ~$138K all-in): **~$186K total tuition / ~$302K all-in (with R&B) over 6 years** — best cost-per-degree on the original board, roughly the same total all-in cost as Columbia 3:2 despite adding a master's.

### Rowing — WashU is a nationally competitive ACRA club

- Student-run club program (not NCAA varsity), competes in **ACRA** (American Collegiate Rowing Association — de facto club national championship; 2025 field: 77 clubs, 388 entries).
- 2024 ACRA: **Men's 4+ national champion**; M8 14th overall (2nd in C Final, mid-pack flagship).
- 2025 ACRA: **Men's 2- bronze in Grand Final**; Women's V8 4th in Grand Final; Women's 2- 6th.
- Pattern: WashU men's small boats race at a genuinely national level. M8 is mid-pack against a deep ACRA field. National-level racing calendar (Charles, Rock, Hooch).
- **Caveat 1 — category:** No lightweight category at ACRA; openweight club racing only. Step down from IRA-system Hobart racing to national-level club racing.
- **Caveat 2 — eligibility (critical):** ACRA enforces its own 4-seasons-in-5-years clock counting prior intercollegiate competition (ACRA 2025 Regatta Packet, Eligibility Rule #2). Jack's 3 Hobart IRA seasons burn 3 of 4 ACRA seasons. He has **1 ACRA season of eligibility remaining when he arrives at WashU** — Y4 only. Y5–Y6 at WashU as a grad student are eligibility-exhausted.

### Course plan summary (WashU Y4–Y6, if confirmed)

**Y1–Y3 at Hobart:** identical to the [path-agnostic Y1–Y2](./hobart-plan.md#hobart-y1y2--path-agnostic-core) + Hobart Y3 in [`hobart-plan.md`](./hobart-plan.md). WashU's prereqs align well with the Hobart Y1–Y3 stack — Calc through multivariable + Diff Eq, Linear Algebra (recommended), Physics 1–2 + 1 upper-division physics (ME-specific), Gen Chem + lab, CS, 15 SH humanities / social science.

**WashU-specific addition:** ME requires "1 semester upper-division physics beyond the required year sequence." Jack's HWS PHYS 270 (Modern Physics) and PHYS 285 (E&M Theory) should satisfy this; confirm with WashU evaluator.

### WashU low-writing elective picks (if confirmed)

- **Humanities (15 SH at WashU Y4–Y5):** History of Science, History of Technology, Engineering Ethics (often offered by McKelvey — likely problem-set / case-study format), Economics Principles. Avoid literature/philosophy seminars.
- **ME electives** (undergrad and grad): MEMS 4410 Vibrations, MEMS 4420 Robotics, MEMS 4500 Control Systems, MEMS 4700 Automotive Engineering — all problem-set / lab / project.
- **MS electives:** most McKelvey grad ME courses are lecture + problem-set + small project. Pick by career interest (energy systems, robotics, aerospace, biomech) rather than writing-load (all are low-writing).
