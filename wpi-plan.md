# WPI Mechanical Engineering BS — course plan (4 years)

*Companion to [`README.md`](./README.md), [`ap-credits.md`](./ap-credits.md) (for the AP score inventory), and [`hobart-plan.md`](./hobart-plan.md) (for the 3 Hobart dual-degree paths). This file covers Option 1 — WPI direct matriculation — term-by-term from Fall 2026 to Spring 2030.*

---

## Structure

4 academic years × 4 seven-week terms (A = Aug–Oct, B = Oct–Dec, C = Jan–Mar, D = Mar–May). Students take **3 courses per term typically**, for 48 course-term slots across the degree (15 units × 3 courses/unit ≈ 45 courses). Each WPI course = 1/3 unit; 15 units total required for the ME BS.

Writing exposure is concentrated in three places: the first-year Inquiry Seminar, the 3-term IQP (social-impact research project, Y3), and the 3-term MQP (senior capstone thesis, Y4).

## WPI HUA electives — low-writing, easy-A picks

Mirrors the approach in [`hobart-electives.md`](./hobart-electives.md): menu + RateMyProfessors (RMP) sentiment + target instructor per slot, so Jack can minimize writing load across the 6-course HUA block.

### How the HUA requirement actually works (recap)

Per the [WPI HUA catalog](https://wpi.cleancatalog.net/humanities-and-arts-requirement): Jack needs **5 student-selected HUA courses + 1 Inquiry Seminar/Practicum (ISP, HU 3900 or HU 3910) capstone = 6 total HUA courses**. Key rules:

- **Depth (3 courses):** 3 thematically-related courses in one disciplinary area (art/music/theatre, literature/writing, history/global studies, philosophy/religion, or one modern language). ≥1 of those 3 must be at the 2000-level or above.
- **Breadth (1 course):** ≥1 HUA course outside the depth grouping. (Exception: 6 courses of one modern language auto-satisfies breadth.)
- **AP cap:** Max 1/3 unit (1 course) of AP credit counts toward HUA. Jack's AP US History = HI 1000 = **1 of his 5 student-selected slots**, and counts as Breadth (History grouping) if depth is anywhere other than History. This means Jack actually enrolls in **4 HUA courses in-term + 1 ISP = 5 WPI term slots** (HI 1000 lands on his transcript at orientation without class attendance).
- **ISP timing:** The ISP is the **capstone** — it's taken **after** the other 5 HUA courses are complete. In the term plan above, all 5 prior HUA land in Y1 A → Y2 A, and the ISP sits in Y2 C.

Goals: (1) minimize total writing, (2) target the 1–2 HUA instructors WPI students consistently flag as "easy A" on RMP, (3) pick a depth area that is project/performance/problem-set-graded rather than essay-graded.

### GPS vs. HUA — Y1 first-term decision

WPI's **Great Problems Seminar (GPS)** is a 2-term A+B first-year sequence that some (but not all) freshmen take. A few GPS sections (Climate Change, Extinctions) carry **INTL 1000** credit and count as HUA; most other sections don't. The term plan above assumes Jack **skips GPS** and goes straight into the Music Tech depth with MU 1100 in Y1 A. If Jack instead enrolls in a HUA-counting GPS section, it would consume the Y1 A + Y1 B slots as 2 HUA courses, replacing MU 1100 and pushing MU 2300 to Y1 D (and shifting the 3000-level courses later). The all-MU sequence below is cleaner for the low-writing goal; re-evaluate at orientation if a specific GPS section looks compelling.

### Breadth — AP US History auto-fills it

Jack's AP US History (4) grants **HI 1000** (1/3 unit). Since Music is in the AR/TH/MU grouping, HI 1000 sits in the History/INTL grouping = different grouping = **breadth requirement satisfied automatically**, at zero coursework and zero writing. This is the #1 reason Music depth is the right pick for Jack: AP US History would otherwise be wasted depth filler if his depth were in History.

### Practical Easy-A hierarchy for Jack's 6 HUA slots

1. **Lock breadth for free** via AP US History → HI 1000 (confirmed on transcript at orientation, Aug 2026).
2. **Take MU 1100 in Y1 A** to unlock the 3000-level MU tech courses. Screen instructor on RMP; avoid Scott Barton sections.
3. **Anchor depth on Manzo** — register for **MU 2300** the first term it's offered with Manzo as instructor (ideally Y1 B). This is the single highest-leverage HUA pick on the WPI roster (1.0/5 difficulty, "easy A" consensus).
4. **Extend depth via Ibbett's MIDI section** — **MU 3301 Topics in MIDI** with Ibbett is the reliable Y1 D 3000-level depth slot.
5. **Flex #4 (Y2 A): pick from MU 3300 / 3302 / 3303** whichever Manzo or Ibbett is teaching; otherwise pick a project-based MU course and screen the instructor on RMP before registering.
6. **Finish with HU 3910 MU Inquiry Practicum in Y2 C** — project deliverable, not a term paper. Target Manzo or Ibbett as practicum advisor if possible.
