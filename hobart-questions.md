# Hobart-path open questions

Consolidated list of every unresolved question across the Hobart paths (Options 2a–2g), grouped by **who can answer it**. Non-Hobart items (WPI, UBC-only, USNA) are out of scope here — those live in [`wpi-plan.md`](./wpi-plan.md) and [`background.md`](./background.md) §9.

Each entry includes a 1-line "why it matters," the target contact, and a link back to the source doc where the full rationale lives. Priority tags:

- **P0** — blocks a near-term commit or application (must answer before enrollment or before the relevant junior-year fork deadline).
- **P1** — shapes the course plan or cost model materially; answer before matriculation or before registration windows.
- **P2** — nice to confirm but doesn't change the strategy.

| 1.3 | **P1** | **7-course AP cap + stacking rules.** Jack's potential pile totals ~14 course units (see [`ap-credits.md`](./ap-credits.md#per-school-mapping)). Confirm (a) whether Physics 1/2 and Physics C stack or only one set counts, (b) whether CS Principles stacks with CS A, and (c) which 7 get prioritized if over cap. Assumes MATH 130 / 131 + PHYS 150 / 160 (or whichever major courses Jack takes) cover Quant Reasoning and Scientific Inquiry. If a gap exists, one free-elective slot must close it. | Gating assumption for the "4 live goals" framing in [`hobart-electives.md`](./hobart-electives.md). |

| 1.7 | **P1** | **Dartmouth Y3 course transfer attributes.** Do non-engineering Dartmouth Y3 courses carry HWS aspirational-goal attributes back onto the HWS transcript? Path 2b only. | Determines whether 3 of Jack's breadth-goal courses can be taken at Dartmouth instead of Hobart — central to the "STEM-heavier 2b" framing in [`README.md`](./README.md#curriculum-fit). |

Re-verify at each registration window that (a) the course runs the proposed term, (b) the goal attribute is still attached in PeopleSoft, (c) the named instructor is teaching. See [`hobart-plan.md` Items to confirm](./hobart-plan.md#-items-to-confirm-with-prof-spector-before-matriculation). | Term-by-term sequencing is brittle if any of these three gates fails. Fallbacks exist in [`hobart-electives.md`](./hobart-electives.md). |

> **Subject:** Dual-degree + AP questions for prospective fall '26 student
>
> Hi Prof. Spector,
>
> My son Jack is looking at Hobart for fall 2026 and is interested in the engineering dual-degree route. He's mainly weighing the Columbia 3:2 and Dartmouth 2:1:1:1, with the Syracuse ECS 4+2 on the radar as a backup MS path. Three things I'm hoping you can help us think through:
>
> 1. **Any engineering partners beyond Columbia, Dartmouth, and Syracuse?** I've seen scattered references to an RPI 3+2 and a WashU 3:3, but neither shows up in the HWS catalogue's Joint Degree Programs section. >

>
> Happy to set up a quick call after mid-July once the Physics C scores are in and we can talk about real numbers — but a short reply or email thread works great too.
>

> Gary [Last name]
