## Working assumption — Physics C both score 5 in May 2026

**The plan assumes Jack scores 5 on AP Physics C: Mechanics AND AP Physics C: Electricity & Magnetism** (sat May 13 and May 14, 2026; scores post mid-July 2026). This is the expected outcome given his 5/5 on algebra-based Physics 1+2 and his STEM track record, and it unifies how every partner school treats his physics credit — calc-based Physics C is on every AP chart we checked, at every Hobart affiliate plus WPI.

If the scores come back lower, the plan needs to be revisited. **Contingency summary** in case that happens:

- **Physics C Mech or E&M < 4** — Jack falls back to Physics 1 + 2 (algebra-based, already 5/5) as his physics AP credit. Downstream impact:
  - **HWS** — Physics 1+2 nominally = 2+2 credits at Physics Dept. discretion, but calc-vs-algebra mismatch against PHYS 150/160 makes an exemption less likely. He'd likely take PHYS 150 + 160 as originally planned in Fall/Spring Y1.
  - **WPI** — Physics 1 and Physics 2 are **not on WPI's AP chart** ([source](https://www.wpi.edu/offices/registrar/policies-procedures/ap-credit)). Jack arrives with **zero physics credit** — must add PH 1110 (Mechanics) + PH 1120 (E&M) and possibly PH 1121 (Waves/Thermo) to Y1. Biggest single schedule impact across all paths. See [`wpi-plan.md`](./wpi-plan.md) for the fallback layout.
  - **Columbia SEAS** — Physics 1+2 = 3 nontechnical elective pts combined, **no exemption** from PHYS UN1401/UN1402 equivalents ([SEAS AP chart p. 27, 2025–26 Planning Guide](https://www.cc-seas.columbia.edu/sites/dsa/files/handbooks/Academic_Planning_Guide_2025-2026_8.14.2025.pdf)). Moot for Combined Plan (HWS's PHYS 150/160 satisfies the prereq).
  - **Dartmouth** — Physics 1+2 **not on Dartmouth's chart** ([source](https://www.dartmouth.edu/reg/enrollment/credit_on_entrance_exemption_charts.html)). Moot for 2:1:1:1 (HWS PHYS 150/160 covers Thayer prereqs).

**Action in mid-July 2026** when scores post: if Physics C both ≥ 4, no change to plan. Otherwise, re-open the WPI term table and the HWS Y1 physics question (does Jack still take PHYS 150/160 or does he defer the whole physics sequence).

## FAQ for the Hobart → Dartmouth (2:1:1:1) path

### Would Dartmouth require Jack to take Physics 1 & 2 anyway?

**No.** Two separate points here:

1. **Dartmouth's intro physics sequence is PHYS 3 + PHYS 4** (calc-based; PHYS 1 and PHYS 2 are not the intro ME sequence). In the 2:1:1:1 path, **Jack takes HWS PHYS 150 + PHYS 160 during his Hobart Y1–Y2**, which articulate to Thayer's physics prereq via the dual-degree agreement. He does NOT take any intro physics at Dartmouth — his Y3 at Dartmouth is ENGS 21–23 + ENGS 33 (solid mech) + ENGS 34 (fluids) + 3 non-ENGS slots, not physics.
2. **His AP Physics 1 + 2 scores (5/5) are unused at Dartmouth.** Dartmouth's AP chart doesn't list Physics 1 or Physics 2 at all — zero credit, zero placement, zero exemption. Only Physics C (assumed 5 per the working assumption above) produces a Dartmouth AP result, and even that is placement-only (see below).

**Bonus nuance:** with Physics C 5/5, Dartmouth grants "Physics 3 credit" + "Physics 4 credit" on entrance, but per Dartmouth policy that credit doesn't count toward the 35-course graduation minimum — placement only. Since Jack is already skipping Physics 3/4 via HWS articulation, the placement is moot.

### Would Dartmouth accept AP US History?

**No.** AP United States History is not on Dartmouth's published AP chart — zero credit, zero placement. Moot for Jack's engineering path anyway because Thayer has no US History requirement. Jack's US History 4 is only useful at WPI (HI 1000 3-credit, fills 1 HUA slot) and HWS (2 general-elective credits).
