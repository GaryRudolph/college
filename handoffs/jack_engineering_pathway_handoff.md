# Jack — Engineering College Pathway Planning

*Handoff document — decision framework, fallback paths, and action items*

---

## 1. Situation Overview

Jack is deciding between two viable starting points for an engineering undergraduate path: Hobart and William Smith Colleges (HWS) with a physics major and a 3-2 engineering dual-degree pipeline, or WPI directly into aerospace engineering. He prefers starting at Hobart. The primary concern is matching engineering ambition to the realistic probability of each pathway succeeding, with clear fallbacks if the preferred path doesn't materialize.

**Stated preferences:**

- BS in engineering is the priority. An MS is acceptable if it's a structural feature of the program (e.g., WashU's 3-3) but not a target in itself.
- Wants to start at Hobart if feasible.
- The primary plan is Hobart physics + Columbia 3-2 engineering dual degree.

---

## 2. Primary Plan — Hobart → Columbia 3-2

Structure: 3 years at Hobart completing physics BA/BS and pre-engineering coursework, followed by 2 years at Columbia's School of Engineering and Applied Science (SEAS). Graduates receive a BA from Hobart and a BS in engineering from Columbia.

### Admission gate

- Columbia requires a minimum **3.50 overall GPA** for affiliates with agreements signed in 2017 or later.
- Admission is competitive review — **not guaranteed** for anyone who began college Fall 2019 or later.
- Pre-engineering GPA ≥ 3.30 required, with minimum grade of B on first attempt in all science and math prerequisites.
- Junior-year application, February deadline; decisions by June.

### Key risk

The 3.50 GPA threshold is the binding constraint. Missing it does not guarantee denial, but moves Jack from "competitive" to "reach" status. This risk drives the entire fallback structure below.

---

## 3. Fallback 3-2 Options (Portfolio Strategy)

Hobart has 3-2 engineering agreements with four partner schools. The smart move is a **portfolio application junior year** — apply to Columbia, RPI, and WashU in parallel. If Columbia's 3.50 doesn't hit, the others almost certainly will.

| Partner | GPA bar | Admission character | Degree outcome | Economics |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Columbia SEAS | 3.50 | Competitive; no guarantees post-2019 | BA + BS (3-2) | Full sticker at Columbia |
| Dartmouth Thayer | ~3.30 | Very competitive | BA + BE (2-1-1-1) | Full sticker |
| WashU McKelvey | 3.25 | ~90% admit; near-automatic if criteria met | BA + BS + MS (3-3) | Automatic 50% tuition discount, 3 years |
| RPI | ~3.00 | Not competitive; high success rate for prepared students | BA + BS (3-2) | Full sticker |

### WashU call-out

WashU is the strongest fallback. Per their FAQ, all applicants are admitted if they meet the 3.25 GPA (overall and math/science), complete prereq courses, and get the institutional endorsement. Barring a felony or academic integrity violation, admission is effectively guaranteed. The structure is 3-3 (BS + MS) rather than 3-2, so Jack would finish with Hobart BA + WashU BS + WashU MS in 6 total years.

### WashU economics

Automatic Graduate Affiliation Scholarship: flat 50% tuition discount for all 3 years at WashU. At 2026-27 rates ($69,190/year graduate tuition), that's ~$34,500/year post-discount × 3 years ≈ **$103,500 at WashU**. The 3-3 BS+MS total cost is roughly $75K less than Columbia 3-2 at full sticker and delivers an additional MS degree.

## 4. Alternative: Dartmouth 2-1-1-1

Different structure from the 3-2 programs: 2 years at Hobart, year 3 at Dartmouth, year 4 back at Hobart (earning BA), year 5 back at Dartmouth (earning BE). Application deadline is **February 1 of sophomore year** — much earlier than the 3-2 programs.

### Timing interaction with 3-2 fallbacks

Dartmouth decisions arrive in mid-April of sophomore year — roughly 10 months before 3-2 applications are due junior year. This means Jack can try for Dartmouth without foreclosing the 3-2 fallback. If rejected in April, he pivots cleanly to the Columbia/RPI/WashU portfolio the next winter.

### Admission is two-step

- **Sophomore-year acceptance** admits him to the Partner School Dual-Degree Program (covers year 3 exchange at Dartmouth). This is a real competitive admission.
- **The BE year 5 requires a separate application** to Thayer (deadline Dec 15 of senior year). Returning Dual-Degree students are automatically admitted provided they apply on time and stay in good standing.

### Performance risk in year 3

This is the biggest caveat. Dartmouth's quarter system (10-week terms) with six ENGS courses in year 3 is demanding. Poor performance compounds downstream:

- **Bs and Cs:** BE admission still administrative. Transcript is mediocre but nothing blocks him.
- **Ds or an E (Academic Risk/Warning):** Thayer may exercise discretion on BE admission. Conversation with the Dual-Degree director required.
- **Suspension/Separation:** BE off the table. Off-ramp becomes finishing Hobart BA + standalone transfer to another engineering school (~2 more years).

**Critical:** the Dartmouth transcript is visible on ALL downstream fallback applications. Unlike the 3-2 path (where only Hobart grades are evaluated), the Dartmouth 2-1-1-1 puts high-stakes engineering grades on his permanent record before the fallback decision happens. Poor Dartmouth performance damages every plan B.

**Pivot rule:** if visibly struggling by end of fall term at Dartmouth, talk to the Dual-Degree director immediately. Early pivot = more options intact.

## 5. Alternative Primary: WPI with Ivy Transfer

If Jack starts at WPI directly in aerospace engineering, he's already in an ABET-accredited engineering program from day 1. Transfer to Columbia or Cornell after 1-2 years is possible but notably harder than the 3-2 pipeline.

### Regular transfer admit rates

- **Columbia overall transfer:** ~5.75–11% (Fall 2023: 187 admitted of 3,254 applicants = 5.75%). SEAS specifically is competitive within that. Minimum 3.50 GPA in practice.
- **Cornell overall transfer:** ~12.6% (834 admitted of 6,581). Engineering runs lower.
- Cornell Engineering is gated on specific prereqs — missing any one (Calc I/II, calc-based Physics I with lab, Engineering Gen Chem with lab) disqualifies the application.
- Cornell allows admission at end of year 1 (sophomore standing) or end of year 2 (junior standing), March 15 deadline.

### Why this is harder than 3-2

The 3-2 cooperative programs exist precisely to avoid the brutal regular-transfer admit rates. Comparing the structural odds:

| Path | Effective admit rate | Predictability |
|---|---|---|
| Columbia 3-2 via Hobart | Competitive review (higher than straight transfer) | Moderate |
| RPI 3-2 via Hobart | High | High |
| WashU 3-3 via Hobart | ~90% if criteria met | Near-automatic |
| WPI → Cornell Engineering (straight transfer) | ~10% or less | Low |
| WPI → Columbia SEAS (straight transfer) | ~5–6% | Very low |

### Strategic heuristic

- **If Ivy is the target:** go to Hobart and work the 3-2 pipeline. RPI and WashU are near-locks; Columbia is reachable.
- **If engineering-from-day-1 is the target:** go to WPI and commit. It's an excellent ABET program; the marginal value of an Ivy nameplate for a working engineer is debatable.
- The mixed path — WPI with Ivy transfer as the plan — combines the weaknesses of both and achieves the worst odds of each.

## 6. Last-Resort Fallback — SDSU Mechanical Engineering

Included for completeness. Only relevant if the entire Hobart 3-2 portfolio fails AND Jack wants the specific ME path. SDSU ME is impacted but accessible.

### Two sub-options

- **Finish Hobart physics BS (4 years), then SDSU BSME (~2 years).** Total 6 years, two degrees, ABET BSME. Expensive (~$70K+ for senior Hobart year on top of SDSU).
- **Transfer out of Hobart after year 2 or 3 directly to SDSU.** Total ~5 years. Forfeits Hobart degree (residency requirement). Cheapest and fastest if engineering is the only goal.

**Honest take:** if all 3-2 partners reject him, paying for a 4th Hobart year to earn a physics BA and then paying for SDSU on top is ~$70K+ for a credential he'll barely use once the ABET BSME is on his resume. Direct transfer is the rational play in that scenario.

## 7. Decision Framework

### Best case — primary plan works

Hobart + Columbia 3-2 if GPA trends 3.5+. Jack gets the dual-degree outcome he's aiming for.

### Most likely good outcome — 3-2 portfolio

Apply to Columbia, RPI, and WashU junior year. RPI or WashU is effectively a lock if he maintains 3.25+. Dual degree outcome preserved, just with a different engineering school.

### Dartmouth as reach

Apply to Dartmouth 2-1-1-1 sophomore year as a reach. Low downside (cheap to apply, early answer by mid-April). If admitted, commit. If rejected, pivot cleanly to junior-year 3-2 portfolio.

### Accept Dartmouth only if confident in year 3 performance

Don't accept Dartmouth 2-1-1-1 on prestige alone. The year 3 transcript damage from poor performance hurts every downstream fallback. If Jack's academic profile suggests C's are likely in six ENGS courses on a 10-week quarter system, the 3-2 route via RPI or WashU is structurally safer.

## 8. Timeline & Action Items

### Before enrolling at Hobart (Spring/Summer)

1. Confirm Dual-Degree liaison at Hobart (currently Prof. Donald Spector, Physics, spector@hws.edu) and get his sign-off on the 4-partner strategy.
2. Pull pre-engineering course requirements for each 3-2 partner and confirm Hobart offers equivalents. Columbia's ME prereqs are strictest; prepping for Columbia covers the others.
3. Clarify with Hobart's registrar: do Dartmouth/partner school grades transfer back into Hobart cumulative GPA, or only credits? (Affects how a bad partner-school year poisons fallback applications.)

### Freshman year at Hobart

1. Take Calc I, Calc II, calc-based Physics I with lab, and introductory programming in year 1. These are prereqs for every 3-2 partner.
2. Maintain 3.5+ cumulative GPA. Aim high — the threshold is the threshold.
3. By December of sophomore year, firm decision on Dartmouth 2-1-1-1 application (due Feb 1 of sophomore year).

### Sophomore year

1. Complete Calc III, Physics II with lab, General Chemistry, remaining programming prereqs.
2. If applying to Dartmouth: submit by Feb 1. Decision mid-April.
3. GPA checkpoint end of sophomore year: if cumulative < 3.25, have a serious conversation about whether direct transfer to an engineering school is cleaner than another year of Hobart tuition.

### Junior year

1. Submit 3-2 portfolio applications: Columbia (mid-Feb), RPI (rolling fall/spring), WashU (Feb 28).
2. If Dartmouth 2-1-1-1 path active: Jack is at Dartmouth for year 3. Monitor performance carefully through fall term.
3. Decisions typically arrive by June of junior year.

### Senior year

1. If on Dartmouth path: submit BE application by Dec 15. Administrative for students in good standing.
2. Complete Hobart graduation requirements.

## 10. Bottom Line

**Recommended posture:** Start at Hobart. Apply to Dartmouth 2-1-1-1 as a sophomore-year reach. Apply to Columbia + RPI + WashU as a junior-year portfolio. The 3-2 structure exists to convert uncertain Ivy admissions into predictable outcomes — use it. WPI is a legitimate alternative primary plan if Jack decides he wants to start engineering from day 1, but it's not a good base for chasing an Ivy transfer later. SDSU is a distant plan D, not plan B.
