## Rowing analysis — this is a major decision factor

**Jack is a lightweight rower.** This matters because dedicated lightweight racing only exists in the U.S. IRA system — not in Canadian university rowing, and not at DIII programs.

### Competition level comparison

**Columbia (after 3:2):** Elite Ivy League lightweight program. Races at Eastern Sprints and IRA Nationals with dedicated lightweight V8, 2V8, and 4+ boats. Was #1 nationally in 2023; won lightweight V8 national championship in 2022. This is the best lightweight destination of the three options.

**Hobart:** DIII school but men's rowing operates in the IRA system, making it Division I-level for rowing purposes. Important recent development: **Hobart announced in August 2025 that starting with the 2026 season, they will compete at the Dad Vail Regatta instead of the NIRC.** The Dad Vail offers 4 automatic qualifier spots to IRA Nationals. Head Coach Chris Kerber said: "The pathway to the National Championship now runs through the Dad Vail Regatta." This is a meaningful upgrade in competition level and ambition. Ranked #24 in D1/D2 IRA coaches poll in 2025. No dedicated lightweight category (openweight only).

**WPI:** NCAA DIII rowing in NEWMAC. Historically strong women's program (2022 NCAA DIII national champions, raced at Women's Henley). Men's program also competes in NIRC but ranked #9 in D3 coaches poll — a step below Hobart. At 2025 NIRC V8 heat: Trinity 5:43, MIT 5:47, Hobart 5:53, Vassar 6:00, WPI 6:02 — Hobart beat WPI by about 9 seconds in the same race.

**UBC:** Canada's best varsity program. Competes at Head of the Charles (24th in 2024 Championship Eights against Cambridge, Olympic medalists, etc.). Won Lightweight 4+ at HOCR in 2022. Seven of last nine Canadian University Rowing Championships. **But:** Canadian university rowing has no dedicated lightweight category. Jack would train openweight.

### Key events explained
- **NIRC (National Invitational Rowing Championship):** Held annually at Lake Quinsigamond, Worcester, MA. Serves as regular-season championship for smaller programs and qualifier for IRA DIII Championships.
- **Dad Vail Regatta:** Largest collegiate regatta in US, held on Cooper River, Pennsauken NJ. As of 2026 season, offers 4 automatic IRA qualifier spots. Hobart is moving here.
- **IRA National Championship:** De facto national championship of US men's rowing since 1895. Includes dedicated lightweight events (V8, 2V8, 4+). Entry is highly competitive via conference championships and at-large bids.

## UBC rowing eligibility for US students

- No nationality restrictions
- U Sports allows **5 years of eligibility** (more generous than IRA)
- Must be enrolled in 3 courses / 9 credit hours during competing term
- Athletic Financial Awards available, capped at tuition + fees (different from NCAA DIII — UBC can offer athletic scholarship)
- No transfer issues since Jack would enter as a freshman
- Main limitation is structural: no dedicated lightweight racing category in Canadian system
